## The Cost of Inaction

Before calculating the ROI of AI adoption, it's important to understand what doing nothing costs.

### Lost Leads

As detailed in our earlier post, law firms lose **30–50% of inbound leads** due to slow response times. For a firm generating 80 leads per month with an average case value of $15,000, that's:

- **40 lost leads per month** at typical conversion rates
- **12 lost signed cases per year**
- **$180,000 in forfeited annual revenue**

### Slow Case Intake

Every day a potential case sits in the review queue is a day the statute of limitations ticks closer. Manual intake processes create a 2–3 week backlog before a new case even reaches an attorney's desk.

### Paralegal Burnout and Turnover

Chronic overwork leads to paralegal burnout. The average annual turnover rate in high-volume PI firms is **40–55%**. Replacing a single experienced paralegal costs **$25,000–$40,000** in recruiting, training, and lost productivity.

### The Compound Effect

These costs compound. A firm losing 12 cases per year to slow response is also losing the referral stream those clients would have generated. A paralegal who leaves takes 6+ months of institutional knowledge with them. Each inefficiency multiplies the others.

| Cost Category | Annual Impact |
|--------------|---------------|
| Lost leads (slow response) | $180,000 |
| Paralegal turnover (replacement) | $30,000 |
| Overtime / burnout costs | $25,000 |
| Delayed case starts (settlement erosion) | $50,000+ |
| **Total Cost of Inaction** | **$285,000+ per year** |
