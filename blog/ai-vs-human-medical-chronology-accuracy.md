## The Three Tiers of Medical Record Review

### Tier 1: Basic OCR

Optical Character Recognition converts scanned documents into searchable text. While useful for keyword searches, basic OCR has fundamental limitations:

- **No context awareness:** OCR reads characters but doesn't understand what they mean
- **Low accuracy on handwriting:** 60–80% on handwritten clinical notes
- **No deduplication:** The same visit documented by two providers is counted twice
- **No chronology assembly:** OCR produces raw text, not organized timelines

**Typical accuracy: 70–85%**

### Tier 2: Human Paralegal Review

Experienced paralegals bring medical knowledge, pattern recognition, and contextual understanding. Under ideal conditions — clean records, single provider, adequate time — accuracy reaches 90–95%.

But humans face inherent limitations:

- **Fatigue effects:** Accuracy drops after 2–3 hours of continuous review
- **Inconsistency:** Different paralegals apply different heuristics
- **Tunnel vision:** Once a paralegal forms a theory of the case, contradictory evidence is more likely to be missed
- **Skipping behavior:** Experienced reviewers learn to "skim" sections they consider routine — and sometimes skip critical details

**Typical accuracy: 85–95%**

## How Reasoning AI Identifies Negligence Markers

Standard OCR and keyword search tools can find the word "fracture" in a document. But they can't tell you whether the fracture was appropriately treated.

Reasoning AI goes several layers deeper by analyzing clinical context across the entire record:

### 1. Temporal Pattern Analysis

The AI maps every clinical event to a timeline and identifies concerning patterns:

- **Delayed diagnosis:** Symptoms reported → diagnosis confirmed: was the gap within standard of care?
- **Gaps in treatment:** Patient discharged → follow-up scheduled: how many days elapsed?
- **Escalation patterns:** Conservative treatment → surgery: was escalation appropriate given the clinical trajectory?

### 2. Protocol Deviation Detection

The AI compares documented care against established clinical protocols:

- **ACS protocols:** Door-to-balloon time >90 minutes for STEMI patients
- **Sepsis protocols:** Antibiotics administered within 1 hour of presentation?
- **Stroke protocols:** CT imaging within 25 minutes of arrival?
- **Surgical checklists:** Was the correct site verified before incision?

### 3. Medication Reconciliation

Polypharmacy and drug interactions are among the most common sources of medical negligence. The AI:

- Cross-references all medications across all providers
- Flags known contraindications
- Identifies dosing errors
- Detects unreconciled medications at discharge

### 4. Communication Breakdown Detection

Many malpractice claims arise from communication failures. The AI identifies:

- **Abnormal results without documented follow-up**
- **Consultation requests that were never fulfilled**
- **Discharge instructions that contradict admitting orders**
- **Radiology findings that were never acted upon**
