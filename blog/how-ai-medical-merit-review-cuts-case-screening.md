### Why Humans Miss Critical Details

Medical records for a single personal injury case can run 500–2,000 pages. Critical information is scattered across emergency notes, radiology reports, operative reports, and discharge summaries from different facilities. A human reviewer must mentally cross-reference dozens of data points.

AI reads every line, compares findings against established protocols, and flags anomalies — all without fatigue, bias, or tunnel vision.

## How AI Medical Merit Review Works: 5-Step Pipeline

### Step 1: Document Ingestion & OCR
Upload PDFs, scans, or digital exports. OCR converts image-based documents to searchable text. **Time: seconds.**

### Step 2: Chronology Extraction
AI extracts every clinical event — dates, diagnoses, treatments, medications — and organizes them into a structured medical timeline in under 60 seconds.

### Step 3: Standard of Care Analysis
AI compares documented care against medical standards — diagnostic protocols, treatment timelines, referral patterns, medication management. Identifies specific deviations as citation-backed findings.

### Step 4: Causation Assessment
Traces the causal chain: deviation → harm → damages. Scores each link for strength of evidence.

### Step 5: Merit Scoring & Report Generation
Outputs overall merit score (1–100), liability score, damages estimate, risk factors, and key findings summary. **Total time: 2–3 minutes.**
