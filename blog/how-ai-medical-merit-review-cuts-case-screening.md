### Why Humans Miss Critical Details

Even the most meticulous paralegal faces cognitive limits. Medical records for a single moderate-to-severe personal injury case can run 500–2,000 pages. Critical information is scattered across:

- **Emergency department notes** (often handwritten or dictated)
- **Radiology reports** (sometimes from different facilities)
- **Operative reports** (with technical surgical descriptions)
- **Progress notes** (daily observations from multiple providers)
- **Discharge summaries** (often delayed by weeks)

A human reviewer must mentally cross-reference dozens of clinical data points to answer three fundamental questions:

1. **Was there a deviation from the standard of care?** (Did the provider act within accepted medical practice?)
2. **Did that deviation cause harm?** (Was there a direct causal link?)
3. **Are the damages significant enough to pursue?** (Does the case economics make sense?)

AI excels at this kind of pattern matching. It can read every line, compare findings against established protocols, and flag anomalies — all without fatigue, bias, or the tunnel vision that sets in after reading 200 similar chart notes.

## How AI Medical Merit Review Works: A Technical Overview

Modern AI medical merit review tools like MeritScan use a multi-layered analysis pipeline that goes far beyond simple keyword searches. Here's how it works:

### Step 1: Document Ingestion and OCR

The process begins when medical records are uploaded — PDFs, scanned documents, faxes, or digital exports. The system applies Optical Character Recognition (OCR) to convert any scanned or image-based documents into searchable text. **Time elapsed: seconds.**

### Step 2: Chronology Extraction

The AI identifies and extracts every clinical event: dates of service, diagnoses, treatments, medications, lab results, and imaging studies. These are organized into a **structured medical chronology** — the same timeline a paralegal would build by hand, but generated in under 60 seconds.

What makes this step powerful is the AI's ability to handle inconsistent date formats, missing information, and records from multiple facilities. It doesn't break when a discharge summary uses a different date format than the ER notes.

### Step 3: Standard of Care Analysis

This is where the real "merit review" happens. The AI compares the care documented in the records against established medical standards:

- **Diagnostic protocols:** Was the appropriate testing ordered given the presenting symptoms?
- **Treatment timelines:** Was treatment initiated within accepted timeframes?
- **Referral patterns:** Were appropriate specialists consulted?
- **Follow-up care:** Was post-discharge monitoring adequate?
- **Medication management:** Were correct dosages administered? Were drug interactions considered?

The AI identifies specific deviations — not as vague "possible negligence" flags, but as **citation-backed findings** with references to the source document and page.

### Step 4: Causation Assessment

A deviation from the standard of care, standing alone, does not make a viable case. The AI must also assess whether the deviation **caused or contributed to** the patient's harm. This requires tracing the causal chain:

> Patient presented with chest pain at 2:00 PM → EKG ordered at 4:30 PM (2.5-hour delay) → STEMI confirmed → Cardiac catheterization at 7:00 PM (beyond the 90-minute door-to-balloon guideline) → Extensive myocardial damage → Permanent heart failure

The AI identifies these causal chains and scores them for strength of evidence.

### Step 5: Merit Scoring and Report Generation

Finally, the AI generates a preliminary merit report with:

- **Overall Merit Score:** 1–100 based on liability strength, damages, and causation
- **Liability Score:** How clear is the deviation from standard of care?
- **Damages Estimate:** Projected economic and non-economic damages
- **Risk Factors:** Potential defenses, contributory negligence, statute of limitations issues
- **Key Findings Summary:** Bullet-point highlights for the reviewing attorney

**Total time for all five steps: 2–3 minutes.**

### What types of medical records can AI process?
Modern AI tools can process any medical document: hospital records, clinic notes, radiology reports, lab results, operative reports, discharge summaries, nursing home records, EMS run sheets, and pharmacy records. PDF, scanned images (via OCR), and digital exports are all supported.

### Is the AI review admissible in court?
The AI-generated merit report is a **screening and case evaluation tool**, not a legal opinion or expert testimony. It helps attorneys decide which cases to accept and how to allocate resources. The report itself is generally not introduced as evidence, though the structured chronology it produces can be used as a litigation work product.

### Can AI replace the need for a medical expert review?
No — and it's not designed to. AI medical merit review is a **screening and triage tool** that helps firms identify the most promising cases and prepare them for expert review. Final case evaluation still requires a qualified medical expert. The AI handles the 80% of grunt work, letting experts focus on the 20% that requires human judgment.
