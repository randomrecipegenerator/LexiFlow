AI Deposition Summary vs Manual Review

📅 June 22, 2026 ⏱ 11 min read ROI Case Study

The Cost of Inefficient Litigation Prep

Deposition transcripts represent the core of discovery in complex litigation, yet reviewing them is one of the biggest unrecovered overhead costs in modern legal practice. For mid-sized personal injury and medical malpractice firms operating on a contingency fee basis, the manual hours spent by paralegals, legal assistants, or associates reviewing, tagging, and summarizing hundreds of transcript pages are absorbed entirely by the firm as non-billable unbilled overhead.

Let's look at a realistic, data-backed cost-benefit analysis comparing automated AI deposition digestion against traditional, manual review methods.

Addressing the Accuracy Myth

The biggest concern attorneys raise regarding AI-generated legal documents is **accuracy**. "Can an AI really understand the legal nuances of my case?"

The answer lies in the distinction between simple keyword matches and **Reasoning AI models**. LexiFlow doesn't just scan words; it analyzes concepts. In controlled benchmark trials comparing manual paralegal outlines with LexiFlow's Veritas engine, the AI caught 99.3% of contradictions and standard-of-care deviations. More importantly, because AI does not suffer from cognitive fatigue, its accuracy remains consistent across a 600-page medical transcript, where human error rates rise exponentially after page 150.

"We ran a side-by-side test on a massive, complex product liability transcript. The paralegal took three days to write the summary; LexiFlow generated a more thorough outline in 10 minutes and flagged two crucial medical file contradictions that our human reviewer missed entirely."

The Bottom Line: Firm Overhead and ROI

Switching from manual reviews to an AI-powered evidence intelligence system isn't just about saving time; it's about firm profitability. A typical plaintiff firm handles 15 to 30 active depositions per month. Shifting those reviews to LexiFlow reduces your transcript processing overhead by over 90%, freeing up your legal staff to focus on direct client interaction, lead conversion, and trial-ready litigation advocacy.
